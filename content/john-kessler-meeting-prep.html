üîÑ Reflexivity & The L1-L5 Model

The Architecture
"I think about agent behavior in five layers:

ENDOGENOUS (inside the agent):
‚Ä¢ L1 ‚Äî Personality: 8 stable traits (Big Five + moral foundations)
‚Ä¢ L3 ‚Äî Political Dispositions: The 132 archetypes ‚Äî where they land on MAT, CSO, CNA, MOR, EPS, PRO
‚Ä¢ L5 ‚Äî Political Behavior: Who they vote for, what they buy, how they act

EXOGENOUS (outside the agent):
‚Ä¢ L2 ‚Äî Formative Experiences: Generation, geography, education, class ‚Äî shapes L1‚ÜíL3
‚Ä¢ L4 ‚Äî Context: What's salient NOW ‚Äî candidates running, issues of the moment, economic conditions ‚Äî transforms L3‚ÜíL5"
Reflexivity in Stock Prices
"In my MoC piece on capital asset pricing, I decompose returns into four forces:

‚Ä¢ Endogenous: Firm execution (margin improvement, product-market fit) ‚Äî PULL dynamics
‚Ä¢ Exogenous: Macro, rates, sector dynamics ‚Äî PULL dynamics
‚Ä¢ Push: Mechanical flows (index inclusion, ETF rebalancing) ‚Äî bypass beliefs entirely
‚Ä¢ Reflexive: When beliefs about price affect fundamentals

The reflexive piece is Soros's insight: A rising stock price ‚Üí easier capital raises ‚Üí better fundamentals ‚Üí higher price. The output feeds back into the input. Most models treat this as noise. It's actually the most interesting signal."
Reflexivity in Agent Simulation
"Same structure applies to Aaru. Agent behavior (L5) changes the context (L4), which reshapes future behavior.

Example: If 40% of simulated consumers switch to a competitor, that competitor gains shelf space and marketing budget (L4 shift), which makes MORE consumers switch. The simulation creates its own future.

Example: If your poll shows Candidate X winning, that changes donor behavior, media coverage, and voter turnout expectations (L4), which changes the actual vote (L5).

This is why static simulation is incomplete. You need temporal dynamics where L5 feeds back into L4. Otherwise you're predicting a world that doesn't account for how predictions change the world."
